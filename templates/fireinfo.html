Fire Evacuation Guidelines

1. Prepare in Advance:

Pack an emergency kit with essentials and other recommended items:
• Food, water, battery-powered radio, flashlight, extra batteries, first-aid kit/medications and masks, important documents, and cell phone with chargers.
Additional Emergency Supplies based on individual needs:
• Prescription medication, glasses and contact solution, infant supplies, pet supplies, feminine hygiene items, sleeping bag/blankets.

2. Plan Evacuation Destinations and Routes:

• Be aware of evacuation destinations where you could seek shelter, such as a friend's or family member's home or a nearby evacuation shelter.
• Identify at least two escape routes from your location to your intended evacuation destination.
For the Elderly and Those With Disabilities: Plan ahead for accessible transportation if needed.

3. Stay Informed:

• Monitor local alerts, weather updates, and fire warnings.

4. Follow Authorities’ Instructions:

If evacuation is ordered, leave immediately with your emergency supply kit.

5. Protect Your Home:

Close and lock doors and windows.
If time allows:
• Unplug electrical equipment like televisions and small appliances.
• Unplug freezers and refrigerators only if there is a risk of flooding.
• If instructed to do so, shut off water, gas, and electricity.
